UAV SPATIAL AI · 2024–2026 · FLAGSHIP

GeoInpainter — Vehicle-free DEM generation for open-pit mines via UAV image inpainting

Heavy equipment captured in UAV surveys distorts photogrammetric terrain models. I built an automated pipeline — YOLOv8 detection → SAM masking → deep inpainting (LaMa / MAT / DeepFillv2) → SfM reconstruction — that removes equipment before DEM generation, and quantified the 2D-quality vs. 3D-geometry trade-off that inpainting introduces into tie-point matching.

MAE 1.276→0.111 m (loader) ~10× faster than manual editing GUI deployed for field use

ROBOT LEARNING · 2025–PRESENT

Mobile manipulation for autonomous mining tasks with vision-language-action models

A mobile manipulator (UGV + 6-DoF collaborative arm, dual RGB-D cameras) trained with imitation learning for autonomous pick-and-place in unstructured settings. By auditing the demonstration dataset with forward-kinematics-based outlier removal, success rate rose to 90% — my current M.S. thesis direction toward perception–decision–manipulation integration in underground mines.

90% pick/place success 95-episode curated dataset 6-DoF arm + dual RGB-D

UNDERGROUND ROBOTICS · 2024

MeshRover Connect — Router-drop teleoperation for communication-denied mines

Underground mines lose all connectivity exactly when robots are needed most. Our quadruped autonomously deploys mesh-network router nodes as it advances, extending its own teleoperation range. Demonstrated 110 m of stable remote operation from the portal of an active limestone mine, with 100% router-drop connection success.

110 m range underground 100% drop-connect success 15 min deployment

FIELD NAVIGATION · 2024 · WHERE IT STARTED

Wheel slip & path error across steering mechanisms in underground rough terrain

My first research project — and the reason I do field robotics. Compared Ackermann, differential, and tracked steering on wet, potholed mine floors using a laser-grid ground truth, in both field experiments and slip-injected simulation. Differential steering recovered paths best (RMSE 0.2311 m); the gap between simulation and reality taught me to never trust a robot that hasn't left the lab.

RMSE 0.231–0.312 m by mechanism field + simulation validation

UAV-AI INSPECTION · 2025 · MOLIT-FUNDED

Drone + AI framework for exterior inspection of high-risk construction machinery

The object-recognition expertise from GeoInpainter, redirected at a new hazard: tower cranes. A three-stage parallel YOLO architecture (ROI selection → part segmentation → defect detection) inspects crane exteriors from UAV imagery, with per-component LLM reporting designed for cost-controlled field deployment.

defect mAP 0.937 8-class ROI mAP 0.852 government R&D grant

About

Toward a Ph.D. in field robotics for mining

I'm an M.S. student at the SMART-X Lab, Department of Energy Resources and Geosystems Engineering, Sejong University (advisor: Prof. Yosoon Choi), working on robotics and automation for mining environments. I started as an undergraduate driving small robots through a wet limestone mine, and have since built systems spanning UAV photogrammetry, deep learning-based perception, GPS-denied SLAM, and robot learning for manipulation.

I'm preparing to apply for Ph.D. programs (Fall 2027) where I can pursue:

  • Autonomous exploration and inspection in GPS-denied underground environments
  • Mobile manipulation and robot learning for mining-equipment automation
  • Planetary surface robotics and ISRU — mining beyond Earth
